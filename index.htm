Hello!

I am currently serving as a Visiting Assistant Professor in the Economics Department at Lafayette College. My academic background includes a Ph.D. in Economics from Culverhouse College of Business at the University of Alabama, along with two master's degrees in Economics from the University of Missouri and Xi'an Jiaotong University, in addition to a Bachelor of Science in Economics from Northwest University of Politics & Law.

My research focuses on Applied Microeconomics,with a specialization in charity economics, experimental economics, public economics, behavioral economics, and industrial organization. I explore intriguing and socially significant topics, including the motivations behind charitable giving and the influence of factors like tax deductibility on donation behavior. To address these questions, I employ diverse research methods such as laboratory experiments, field experiments, structural modeling of field data, and meta-analysis..

Beyond my academic pursuits, my interests extend into the realm of personal well-being. I find solace in the rhythm of a jog, enrichment in the pages of a book, and tranquility in the practice of yoga.

I will be on the job market in 2023-2024.
